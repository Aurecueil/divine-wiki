# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want the Divine Skins community to be welcoming to everyone. That means no harassment, no hate, no bullying — on GitHub, in Discord, or anywhere connected to this project.

We pledge to make taking part in the project a respectful experience for everyone, no matter your age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity.

## Our standards

Things that help the community:

- Use welcoming and kind language.
- Respect different opinions and experiences.
- Take feedback well.
- Focus on what's best for the community.
- Show empathy to others.

Things that are not okay:

- Sexual language or imagery, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Trolling, insults, or personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Sharing other people's private info (address, email, etc.) without permission.
- Anything you wouldn't do at a friend's house.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Maintainers decide what counts as okay behavior. They will take fair action when they see something off.

Maintainers can edit, remove, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that don't match this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces. It also applies when you represent the project in public — using an official email, posting from an official social account, or showing up as a project rep at an event.

## Enforcement

Saw something? Email `conduct@divineskins.gg`.

All reports will be reviewed and looked into. We will respect the privacy and safety of the person who reported.

## Consequences

Maintainers will follow these steps when deciding what to do about reported behavior:

### 1. Correction

**What it looks like:** Language or action seen as not professional or not welcome.

**What happens:** A private written warning from a maintainer. You'll get a clear note about what was wrong and why. A public apology may be asked for.

### 2. Warning

**What it looks like:** A single incident, or a pattern.

**What happens:** A warning, with rules about future behavior. No contact with the people involved — including unsolicited messages — for a set time. This covers Discord, GitHub, and any outside channels. Breaking these rules may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary ban

**What it looks like:** A serious break of community standards. Repeated bad behavior counts.

**What happens:** Banned from any contact or public talk with the community for a set time. No public or private contact with the people involved during the ban. Breaking the ban may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent ban

**What it looks like:** A pattern of breaking community standards. Includes harassment of a person, or aggression toward a group.

**What happens:** Banned from all public talk inside the community, forever.
