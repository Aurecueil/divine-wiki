### What You're Working With

League of Legends stores its audio in a few file types you'll meet again and
again:

- `.bnk`: a Wwise "SoundBank" that holds audio data and/or the logic and event
  hierarchy.
- `.wpk`: a Riot-specific format that stores only raw audio (no metadata).
- `.wem`: the individual compressed audio format Wwise uses internally.

<Callout type="info" title="Which file you edit">
  For **SFX** (abilities, impacts, UI sounds), you'll usually edit an
  `audio.bnk` file.

For **VO** (voice lines, announcer), you'll usually edit an `audio.wpk` file.

</Callout>

---

### Finding the Right Files

You need three files to edit a character's sounds:

| File               | Example path                                                                          |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Audio (wpk or bnk) | `ASSETS/Sounds/Wwise2016/VO/en_US/Characters/Yone/Skins/Base/Yone_Base_vo_audio.wpk`  |
| Events bank        | `ASSETS/Sounds/Wwise2016/VO/en_US/Characters/Yone/Skins/Base/Yone_Base_vo_events.bnk` |
| Bin file           | `data/characters/Yone/skins/skin0.bin`                                                |

You can load these files into Quartz directly, or use one of the preset options:

- **From game.** Select a champion and their skin (leftmost option).
- **From an existing mod.** If you're editing, sourcing, or building on top of
  another mod, load the mod's folder instead (second option from the left).

### Step-by-Step: Replacing a Sound

**1. Open the Sound Bank tab in Quartz.**

<img src="/wiki-images/quartz-empty-2.png" alt="Empty Quartz Sound Bank tab" />

**2. Load your files on the LEFT side.**

Click the left panel and point Quartz to your `.bin`, audio (`.bnk` or `.wpk`),
and events (`.bnk`) files. Then hit **Parse**.

This populates the left side with a list of all sounds in that bank.

<img src="/wiki-images/quartz-2-2.png" alt="Parsed bank on the left panel" />

You can have multiple banks parsed and loaded on the left at the same time.

**3. Add your replacement sounds on the RIGHT side.**

The right panel is where you load the new audio you want to use. Quartz accepts:

- `.wav`
- `.wem`
- `.ogg`
- `.mp3`

It handles format conversion automatically. You do not need to convert to
`.wem` before importing.

You can also load another parsed League audio bank on the right side and pull
sounds from it directly.

<img
  src="/wiki-images/quartz-3-2.png"
  alt="Replacement sounds on the right panel"
/>

**4. Drag to replace.**

Drag a sound from the **right panel** onto the sound you want to replace in the
**left panel**. The entry on the left updates to reflect the replacement.

<img
  src="/wiki-images/quartz4.png"
  alt="Dragging a sound to replace it in Quartz"
/>

**5. Save your edited bank.**

Select your bank on the left panel, then click **"Save as BNK/WPK"** and save it
somewhere in your mod folder.

<img
  src="/wiki-images/quartz-save-2.png"
  alt="Saving the edited bank in Quartz"
/>

### What to Include in Your Mod

| Mod type | Files to include                 |
| -------- | -------------------------------- |
| SFX mod  | `audio.bnk` only                 |
| VO mod   | `audio.wpk` **AND** `events.bnk` |

For VO mods, the events bank is required because it defines which audio file
plays for each in-game event (attack, death, joke, etc.). Without it, your new
audio won't be triggered correctly.
